HAPPY 39TH BIRTHDAY, LEO MESSI

Today — June 24, 2026 — Lionel Messi turns 39. He's celebrating with the all-time World Cup scoring record: 18 goals across six tournaments, surpassing Miroslav Klose (men's, 16) and Marta (women's, 17). The greatest birthday gift in football history.

ALL-TIME WORLD RECORD

Messi surpassed Klose's men's record (16) and Marta's all-record (17) in the same brace vs Austria (June 22). He now holds the greatest individual scoring record across all FIFA World Cups — men's and women's combined.

⚡
Cristiano Ronaldo  ·  🇵🇹 Portugal
FIRST TO SCORE AT 6 DIFFERENT WORLD CUPS

No player in history has ever scored at six World Cups — until June 23, 2026. Ronaldo's brace vs Uzbekistan (Portugal won 5-0) made him the first and only: 2006 · 2010 · 2014 · 2018 · 2022 · 2026.

🎖️
Cristiano Ronaldo  ·  Age 41
2ND OLDEST WC SCORER IN HISTORY

At 41 years and 138 days, only Roger Milla (42y 39d, Cameroon 1994) has scored at a World Cup at an older age. Ronaldo scored twice. Critics said he was done — he answered with a brace in a 5-0 win.

🔥
Lionel Messi  ·  WC2026 Form
5 GOALS · ARGENTINA INTO THE KNOCKOUTS

Hat-trick vs Algeria (June 16). Brace vs Austria (June 22). Messi is the WC2026 top scorer and Argentina — the defending world champions — are through to the knockout rounds with their captain in devastating form at 39.
